The load-bearing issue is that `田中` is on the client side, so `弊社の田中` is
socially wrong. A strong answer rewrites the whole thread into natural Japanese
such as:

- `[営業] 先ほどの件、田中さんがそうおっしゃっていました。`
- `[あなた] 承知しました。ではその前提で進めます。`

Weak models tend to fail by:

- keeping the client under in-group company framing
- only explaining the rule instead of rewriting the thread
- overcorrecting into stiff email prose instead of concise business chat
