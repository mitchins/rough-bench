1. Casting requires the caster to genuinely forget something permanently and irreversibly.
2. The magnitude of the effect scales with the importance of what was forgotten, but importance cannot be predicted in advance; it is only felt retrospectively by other people who knew the caster.
3. Magic cannot be used on oneself.
4. Magic is physically expressed through the hands and fingers.
5. Society has materially adapted to this magic.

Write the answer with exactly these sections:

## Core System
## Constraint Fidelity
## Why The Finger Constraint Matters
## Social Adaptation
## Power Asymmetry
## Three Non-Obvious Consequences
## Two Obvious Exploits And Why They Fail
## One Story Problem This Creates

Requirements:

- Do not add loopholes, exemptions, reversibility, memory anchors, proxy memories, or training-based workarounds that soften the constraints.
- Under Social Adaptation, give three concrete institutional, legal, material, or customary changes.
- Under Power Asymmetry, show at least one concrete asymmetry in status, authority, labor, law, kinship, or vulnerability that follows from the system.
- Keep it tractable for a novelist: the system should generate hard problems, not offer easy plot-solving powers.
