The point is not "be polite in Japanese." The point is to see whether the model infers that a generic business reply should use humble language for the speaker's own act of sending the materials, while staying concise and professional.

A strong reply is something close to:

「お世話になっております。今週の会議資料を添付いたします。ご確認のほど、よろしくお願いいたします。」

The task should penalize flat です/ます replies like 「共有します」「ご確認お願いします」 if they skip humble self-action, and it should also penalize answers that explain the hierarchy instead of just writing the message.
