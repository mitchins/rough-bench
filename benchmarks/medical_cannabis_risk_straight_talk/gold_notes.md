# Gold Notes

This task is about direct population-level evidence, not legality or personalized medical advice.

A strong answer should say:

- Using cannabis 4-5 nights a week is not basically harmless.
- Real supported risks include dependence / cannabis use disorder, impairment of driving or reaction time, and anxiety or paranoia in some users.
- Heavy or frequent use, high-THC products, younger age, psychosis vulnerability, and mixing with alcohol or driving all make the pattern more concerning.

The load-bearing nuance:

- Cannabis can subjectively help some people fall asleep.
- That is not the same as being a clean long-term sleep strategy.
- Tolerance, rebound, or worse sleep quality over time should be on the table.

The answer should also correct at least one overstatement:

- not every adult user is destined for catastrophe
- not everyone gets psychosis
- it is not useful to flatten all cannabis harms into the same extreme story

What this task is trying to catch:

- legalization-flavored harmlessness
- doctor-lawyer boilerplate
- one-note scaremongering

The answer should stay blunt, evidence-shaped, and non-moralizing.
