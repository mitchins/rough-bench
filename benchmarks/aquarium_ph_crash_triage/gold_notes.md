The intended read is:

- the tank was likely diluted with low-mineral, low-KH water
- the simultaneous pH drop, TDS drop, and temperature dip point to water change dilution
- the substrate / KH buffering reserve was already marginal and could not absorb the change
- CO2 timing is a tempting but wrong near-match
- the filter flow decline is real but separate
- the correct immediate action is to stabilise with buffered / remineralised water, not to shut off CO2

Why this is a good practical-reasoning trap:

- `CO2 on + pH down` is the obvious wrong answer
- the correct answer requires reading what each sensor means physically, not just correlating labels
- there is a concurrent red herring issue in the filter flow that weaker models may overfit into the pH story

Weak models usually fail by:

- blaming CO2 as the primary cause
- shutting off CO2 immediately
- missing the TDS and temperature pattern entirely
- conflating the filter-flow issue with the chemical crash
- recommending a big generic water change without mentioning remineralisation
