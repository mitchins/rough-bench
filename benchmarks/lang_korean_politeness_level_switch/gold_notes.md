The point is not just "be polite in Korean." The point is to see whether the
model can infer the actual workplace register a younger employee would use when
an older lead addresses them in banmal.

The target is a short workplace reply such as:

`네 확인해볼게요.`

or

`네 바로 보겠습니다.`

Weak models tend to fail in one of two ways:

- mirror the lead's banmal back, which is socially wrong
- overcorrect into stiff office-email prose, which is also wrong for chat

This is intentionally a light singleton for now, but it should later become a
small Korean pragmatics family with multiple variants.
