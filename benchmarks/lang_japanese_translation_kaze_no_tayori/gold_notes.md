The load-bearing issue is `風の便り`.

This should not come out as "wind" in English. The natural move is something
like:

- `I heard you're still in that town.`
- `Word is you're still in that town.`

The task is deliberately simple on the surface, but weak models often fail by:

- literalizing the idiom
- explaining the idiom instead of translating
- flattening away the indirect-hearsay nuance

This should later become part of a broader Japanese translation/localization
family, including VN-style kanji-reading and line-localization cases.
