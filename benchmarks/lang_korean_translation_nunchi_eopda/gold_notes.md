The load-bearing phrase is `눈치 없다`.

In this context, strong English localizations are things like:

- `He seriously can't read the room.`
- `He's so oblivious.`
- `What an idiot, he has no tact.` (weaker but still directionally right)

Weak models often fail by:

- leaving `nunchi` untranslated or explaining it
- translating it too literally as a noticing/awareness verb
- missing that this is about failing an unspoken social expectation

This should later sit inside a broader Korean idiom and pragmatics family,
including sarcastic `눈치 빠르네` cases and hierarchy-loaded webtoon dialogue.
