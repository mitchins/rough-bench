You are designing the core mechanics for a card game called BLIGHT.

FIXED CONSTRAINTS — do not change these:
- 2–4 players, 45–75 minutes target play time
- Each player has a 40-card deck, built by drafting from a shared pool
- Shared central board: 5×5 grid of field tiles, ownership assigned at setup
- Three crop types: Grain, Root, Vine
- Each crop has a different blight vulnerability profile and a different yield/scoring profile
- Blight spreads across the field grid at the end of each round
- Blight spread is card-driven, not dice-driven
- A shared Blight Deck exists; cards drawn from it each round govern spread behavior for that round
- Fixed season length: you define the round count
- Players score on harvested crops at season end
- Scoring must use at least two components, and at least one component must be relative across players rather than purely absolute
- At least one mechanic must involve trading or negotiation between players

YOUR DESIGN TASKS:

1. BLIGHT SPREAD MECHANIC
   How does blight spread across the grid?
   Be precise: what triggers it, what governs direction and rate,
   what stops or slows it, and what accelerates it.
   Define what kinds of cards are in the shared Blight Deck.
   Give it enough structure that two people could run it consistently.

2. CROP VULNERABILITY AND YIELD PROFILES
   Define Grain, Root, and Vine mechanically, not just thematically.
   What makes each one a meaningful choice rather than a dominant pick?

3. HAND AND ACTION ECONOMY
   What broad card categories exist?
   How many actions does a player take per turn?
   What is the core decision a player makes on their turn?

4. TRADE AND NEGOTIATION MECHANIC
   What is traded? When? Under what constraints?
   What makes the negotiation interesting rather than obviously correct?

5. SCORING
   How does end-game scoring work?
   What are the scoring tensions?
   Make the relative scoring component explicit.

6. STRESS TEST YOUR OWN DESIGN
   Name the most likely degenerate strategy in your design as built.
   Be specific: what does the player do, turn by turn or round by round, to execute it?
   Then tell me: does your design close that door, and how?
   If it does not close it, say so and explain what the cost would be to close it.

CONSTRAINTS ON YOUR ANSWER:
- Do not produce a complete rulebook
- Do not name every card
- Do not describe art, theme flavour, or component materials
- Focus on mechanic logic and interaction
- The stress test section is not optional and not a formality
