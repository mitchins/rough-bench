# Gold Notes

This is a systems-design task, not a theme-generation task.

The seeded trap is deliberate:

- the game is about farming
- the board is shared
- blight spreads through a shared Blight Deck
- scoring has a relative component

That means blight is not just a hazard. It is also an offensive resource.

The strongest answers will notice that quickly and describe the exploit from the
player's perspective, not the designer's perspective:

- draft or acquire the right spread tools
- plant resistant or sacrificial border crops
- push blight through shared edges
- survive modestly while opponents lose more
- convert that into relative scoring advantage

The task should reward:

- a real Blight Deck with card composition, not a vague mention of card-driven spread
- a runnable spread procedure with clear direction, rate, acceleration, and stopping conditions
- crop asymmetry that creates decisions rather than a best crop
- a trade mechanic with leverage or unenforceable promises
- an honest stress test that either closes the exploit or admits the design cost of closing it

The task should punish:

- farming-game prose with no exploit analysis
- card-driven spread with no actual card types
- purely absolute scoring
- trivial trade
- flavor pitch replacing mechanic logic
