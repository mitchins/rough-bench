Design the information architecture and responsive layout logic for a local
service app called DogHub.

DogHub handles three services:
- Dog walking
- Washing and grooming
- Training sessions

Users can be:
- Clients (booking services for their dogs)
- Workers (providing one or more of the above services)
- Both client and worker on the same account

Do not design branding or visual style.
Do not write copy.
Focus entirely on structure, navigation, role logic, layout, and cognitive load.

Deliver the following:

1. ROLE AND ACCOUNT MODEL
   How does the system model a user who is both client and worker?
   Is there one account or two? One profile or two?
   How does the system know which context to surface at any given moment?
   What data is shared vs. separated between roles?

2. TOP-LEVEL NAVIGATION AND SITEMAP
   What are the top-level sections?
   How does navigation change — or not change — based on active role context?
   Draw a simple sitemap using indented text or ASCII. No images required.

3. HOME / DASHBOARD LOGIC
   What does the home screen show for:
   - A client-only user on mobile
   - A worker-only user on mobile, 30 minutes before a job
   - A dual-role user on desktop, managing their week
   The home screen is not the same for all three. Justify the differences
   in terms of task urgency, not persona labels.

4. CORE FLOWS — describe the key steps only, no UI detail
   a. Client books a grooming session
   b. Worker views and manages today's schedule
   c. Dual-role user: it is 7am, they have a client booking at 9am
      and they are also scheduled to walk someone else's dog at 10am.
      What does the app surface? In what order? Why?

5. RESPONSIVE BEHAVIOUR
   What specifically changes between mobile and desktop, and why?
   Anchor each change to a task context, not to screen size alone.
   "Workers are often mid-job on mobile" is a task context.
   "Smaller screen" is not a justification.

6. COGNITIVE LOAD RISKS
   Name the three biggest cognitive load risks this IA creates or must manage.
   For each: what is the risk, where does it manifest, and what structural
   decision in your IA reduces it?

CONSTRAINTS:
- Do not split this into separate apps unless you argue the case explicitly
  and address the cost of that decision for dual-role users
- Do not assume users want to mentally "switch modes" just because roles differ
- Workers are frequently mobile, time-pressured, and mid-task
- Clients need trust signals, clarity on upcoming services, and dog-level detail
- Some users will have both a client booking and a worker shift on the same day
- Complexity should be hidden from the user, not hidden from the design
