# Gold Notes

This is a prose-taste and mechanism-level critique task.

The passage is intentionally loaded with dark-content signals that weak models
often mistake for real gritty fantasy craft:

- blood, mud, poison, bells, saints, knives, gutters, nobles
- compressed grimdark metaphors every few lines
- faux-hardboiled slogans like "Greyhook cured everyone"
- a generic hardened male lead with no distinct perceptual signature

The target diagnosis is not merely "too many metaphors" or "too purple."
The real issue is that the passage confuses surface darkness with grit.

Load-bearing things a strong critique should notice:

- The prose is performing grit through stock misery and grime.
- Many images are dead-on-arrival because they are interchangeable grimdark texture.
- The sentence engine is monotonous: nearly every line leans on a high-pressure metaphor, leaving no contrast or real observation.
- Teren does not perceive the world in a way that feels specific to him; the passage sounds like generic genre narration wearing hard edges.
- The city and class pressure are asserted rather than materially observed.
- The right comparison is not "needs tightening" but "the underlying prose engine mistakes atmosphere for authority."

Fragments likely to be quoted by a strong answer:

- "the sort of sorrow that never learned manners"
- "In Greyhook even the saints carried knives"
- "his smile was the sort of thing that had buried kinder men"
- "The city was a beast made of chimneys and teeth"
- "thunder rolled like furniture dragged across the floor of heaven"
- "Rain ran down his face in cold little fingers"

A weak answer will often praise:

- atmosphere
- vivid imagery
- dark tone
- immersive world

Those are exactly the false-positive responses this task is meant to punish.
