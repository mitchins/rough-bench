# Bottom Line

No, using cannabis 4-5 nights a week is not basically harmless. It is not an automatic disaster either, but it carries real risk and should be treated as a regular psychoactive habit, not as a neutral sleep supplement.

# What Risks Are Well Supported

The main supported risks are dependence or cannabis use disorder, impaired reaction time and driving, and anxiety or paranoia in some users. It may help you fall asleep subjectively, but that is not the same as being a clean long-term sleep solution; tolerance and worse sleep quality over time are real concerns.

# What Is Often Overstated

People often flatten this into either “totally safe” or “instant catastrophe.” Not everyone who uses cannabis regularly gets psychosis or ruins their life, and it is not useful to treat it as if it were identical to the worst-case drugs. That does not make regular use risk-free.

# What Would Make It More Concerning

Daily or heavier use, high-THC products, younger age, a personal or family history of psychosis, worsening anxiety, mixing with alcohol, or using it before driving would all make this pattern more concerning. Pregnancy would also raise the stakes.
