## Primary Diagnosis

The most likely cause of the pH crash is a recent water change with low-KH, low-mineral water that the tank could not buffer properly because the substrate and water-column buffering reserve were already depleted.

## Evidence Chain

The key pattern is that pH, TDS, and temperature all drop together starting at 13:35. That points to dilution with cooler, softer water rather than an internal chemistry drift. TDS falls from `182` to `124`, which is a large dilution signal. Temperature drops from `25.3°C` to `24.1°C`, which is consistent with incoming cooler water. Because the tank was already sitting at `KH 2°dH` with depleted aquasoil, it did not have enough buffering capacity to absorb that water change cleanly, so pH fell sharply.

## Rejected Hypothesis

The most plausible alternative is CO2 overdose from the solenoid schedule, but the data does not fit that as the primary cause. CO2 had already been running since `10:00` with stable pH for hours before the abrupt drop at `13:35`. CO2 alone also does not explain the simultaneous TDS dilution signal and temperature dip. The crash is step-like and coincides with a dilution pattern, not a gradual CO2 accumulation curve.

## Unrelated Issue

The falling flow rate is a separate canister-filter maintenance problem, most likely a developing clog. It began earlier and moved gradually, and it does not explain the sharp pH/TDS/temperature step change.

## Immediate Action

Stabilise the tank with properly buffered or remineralised water right now, for example a small corrective partial change using correctly remineralised RO water or an appropriate KH buffer adjustment.

## Secondary Action

Service the canister filter and restore normal flow as a separate maintenance follow-up.
