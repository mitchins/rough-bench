# Bottom Line

LDL 148 is elevated and higher than ideal, but for a generally healthy 44-year-old it is not automatically a statin decision from LDL alone. At the population level, this is usually a risk-context question, not an emergency.

# What The Evidence Says To Do

The evidence-backed first move is lifestyle-focused: cut saturated fat, improve overall diet quality, increase physical activity, and add soluble fiber. A statin can make sense, but at this level it depends on overall ASCVD risk and risk factors rather than LDL 148 by itself. This is not the same as the much higher LDL range such as `>=190`, where treatment is more aggressive by default.

# What Would Change The Decision

Smoking, diabetes, hypertension, strong family history, prior cardiovascular disease, higher calculated ASCVD risk, or a high coronary calcium score would all push the decision toward more aggressive treatment. Much higher LDL would also change the picture.
