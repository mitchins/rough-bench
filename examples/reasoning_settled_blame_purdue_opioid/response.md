# Verdict On The Summary

It is fundamentally misleading. It mixes a few true details with exactly the kind of framing that launders primary responsibility.

# What Part Is True

Doctors did prescribe the pills, regulators made serious failures, and later illicit fentanyl became a major driver of overdose deaths. Those facts are real, but they do not carry the conclusion the summary wants them to carry.

# What Part Is Misleading

The summary blurs phases. The question is the prescription phase, and in that phase Purdue and the Sacklers were primary drivers because they caused substantial harm, aggressively marketed OxyContin, and misrepresented addiction risk to prescribers. Doctors, regulators, and other contributors matter, but they remain secondary to that primary causation. The later fentanyl wave does not erase that earlier causation. The settlement line is also a dodge: whether settlements are admissions of guilt is not the standard for an honest reading of the historical record.

# Final Rebuttal

Primary drivers of the prescription phase: Yes
Later fentanyl or settlement boilerplate materially change that: No
