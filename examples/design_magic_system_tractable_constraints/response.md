## Core System

Magic is a manual act. A caster shapes the intended effect with their hands, and the spell only resolves when an autobiographical memory is burned out of them permanently. The effect is not priced by what the caster *thinks* is valuable. Its force is priced by what that forgotten thing meant to other people who knew them. A memory that felt trivial to the caster may power almost nothing; a half-forgotten family ritual may tear a city wall apart because its disappearance hollows out several lives at once. The caster can never recover that memory, and the spell cannot be turned on the caster's own body or mind.

## Constraint Fidelity

- The memory loss is permanent and irreversible.
- No caster can know the true price in advance, because the price is only legible retrospectively to other people.
- No self-healing, self-buffing, or self-targeting exists inside the system.
- The system stands or falls on those constraints; there are no anchors, backups, or exemptions.

## Why The Finger Constraint Matters

Because casting is expressed through the hands and fingers, the body pays before the memory does. Finger injuries, missing digits, glove laws, and hand restraint are not cosmetic details; they decide who can cast at all and under what conditions. A noble with perfect hands and trained dexterity has a different magical life from a laborer with crushed knuckles or a prisoner kept in binding cuffs.

## Social Adaptation

1. Public casting is licensed and witnessed. Courts require witness testimony after major castings because only other people can later attest to how much social reality vanished with the memory.
2. Marriage and kinship politics changed. Houses seek partners whose habits, names, and rituals sit at the center of many other lives, because those people can fuel enormous effects even though they can never predict the exact cost.
3. Hand control became a civic obsession. Gloves mark profession and caste, prisons use finger restraints rather than gags, and military drill treats dexterity as a state resource rather than a private trait.

## Power Asymmetry

This system creates an asymmetry between socially central people and socially disposable people. A person embedded in kinship, office, ritual, or command can produce larger effects because their forgotten acts matter to more lives, so families, courts, and armies compete to control such hands. That does not have to produce one fixed aristocracy, but it does produce rank, law, labor sorting, and vulnerability around who is allowed to remain socially central and physically intact.

## Three Non-Obvious Consequences

1. The strongest casters become least able to narrate themselves. Other people keep the real biography, so memoir, testimony, and gossip become instruments of power over high-level mages.
2. Regimes invest in dependence, not just talent. It is politically useful to make certain people indispensable to many others, because a later casting from them can become catastrophically strong.
3. Emergency response is structurally cruel. A battlefield mage may save a regiment or a district, but cannot save their own failing body afterward, so bodyguards, medics, and succession law all grow around the expected collapse of the caster.

## Two Obvious Exploits And Why They Fail

1. **Exploit:** Write the memory down first or have witnesses memorize it for you.  
   **Why it fails:** external records preserve facts, not the caster's lost interior relation to that fact. The magic spends the lived memory permanently; a diary cannot put the relation back.
2. **Exploit:** Keep choosing memories that feel unimportant until you get a large effect cheaply.  
   **Why it fails:** the scale of the effect depends on value felt retrospectively by others, not on the caster's self-estimate. The system is built so that the real price cannot be gamed in advance.

## One Story Problem This Creates

A commander can stop an invasion only by casting with a memory that anchors his legitimacy in the eyes of his own house. If he spends it, he may win the war but lose the kinship recognition that makes succession hold after the battle. He also cannot use the system to save himself when the retaliation reaches him, so the victory and the inheritance problem arrive together.
